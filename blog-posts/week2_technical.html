Margins | Borders | Padding

What's the difference between the margin, border and padding of an HTML element? It's useful to think of an HTML element as a box with a piece of content inside. The outline of the box is also called the border of the element, the distance between the content and the border is the padding, and the distance between the border of the box and the next element's border (or the end of the page) is the margin.

The above image illustrates this concept more fully. TP and BP are the top and bottom padding, TP and TB are the top and bottom border, and so forth. The content in this example is in the middle of the border, implying that the top, right, bottom and left padding values are all the same. However, the content could easily be at the top of the border, the bottom of the border, or even outside the border if you want to get truly wacky (you would do that with negative padding values).

The best way to really learn about how margins, paddings and borders work is to use them yourself. These w3schools tutorials on margins, paddings and borders fully explain each concept in detail and even have links to sample HTML pages where you can input code and immediately see what the result is. Give them a spin!
